United Nations accredited non-government organisations and individuals working for social change

The NGO Committee for the Elimination of Racism, Acrophobia & Colorism formerly known as the Subcommittee for the Elimination of Racism is a stand alone committee.  
We continue to  promote human rights, social justice and peace at local, national, regional and international levels. As advocates against human rights violations, we develop, facilitate and implement educational programs and global policies to eliminate the inter-generational and contemporary impact of Afrophobia, racism, racial discrimination (systemic and structural), xenophobia and related intolerance in accordance with the United Nations Charter, the International Convention for the Elimination of all forms of Racial Discrimination, the Durban Declaration & Programme of Action Plan, the Convention for the Rights of the Child, the Convention for the Elimination of  All forms of Discrimination Against Women, and the UN General Assembly's Programme of
Activities for the International Decade for People of African Descent 2015-2024.  Our work is informed by the United Nations'  human rights principles and is approached from multidisciplinary and intersectional perspectives.  
Do email us if you are interested in becoming a member of the CERAC.       

​​

About Us

The NGO Committee for the Elimination of Racism, Afrophobia and Colorism (CERAC) serves as a forum which collects, receives, shares, and disseminates information on human rights norms and standards; policies and best practices dedicated to the elimination of the scourge of Afrophobia, racism, racial discrimination, xenophobia and related intolerance. We raise awareness of human rights frameworks intended to encourage prejudice free societies, including but not limited to eradicating accompanying intersectional issues of gender, spiritual, epistemological, juridical, digital, social, and economic inequities.
